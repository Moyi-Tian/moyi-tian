Math Related

  • Mentor at BUMP (Brown Undergraduate Mathematics Projects) (Summer 2020)

    • Guided a team of 3 undergraduate students in a 5-week directed study in experimental mathematics on the topic of graphing fleas

    • Given a preset rule on the flea's motion, the team explored possible patterns of the trajectories on different lattices by running numerical simulations in Python. By the end, they wrote up a report addressing their findings, conjectures, and proofs, and then gave a public presentation to other teams in the program.

  • Translated the German article, Knoten, by Horst Schubert (1968) for my final project in German for Reading class (Summer 2020) See it here!

  • Mentor in the Brown Applied Math Undergraduate/Graduate Mentoring Program (From Fall 2019)

    • The program pairs undergraduates who are interested in studying applied math with graduate students. The goal is to provide undergraduates with an additional advising perspective, particularly as it pertains to academic and career goals. I met and had conversations with my mentee on a regular basis throughout the semesters.​
